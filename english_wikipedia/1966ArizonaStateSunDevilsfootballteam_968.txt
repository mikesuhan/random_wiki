The  1966 Arizona State Sun Devils football team  was an  American football  team that represented  Arizona State University  in the  Western Athletic Conference  (WAC) during the  1966 college football season . In their ninth season under head coach  Frank Kush , the Sun Devils compiled a 5–5 record (3–2 against WAC opponents), finished in a tie for second place in the WAC, and were outscored by their opponents by a combined total of 174 to 166. [1] [2]

The team's statistical leaders included John Goodman with 1,259 passing yards, Travis Williams with 551 rushing yards, and Ken Dyer with 496 receiving yards. [3]
