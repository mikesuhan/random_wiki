The Al Yahudu tablets are an extra-biblical archive from the exiled  Judean  community in  Babylonia  in the 6th and 5th centuries BCE. They contain information on the physical condition of the exiles from Israel and their financial condition in  Babylon .

The published book has been announced by David I. Owen, Editor-in-Chief of Cornell University Studies in Assyriology and Sumerology (CUSAS) at  Ithaca College .
