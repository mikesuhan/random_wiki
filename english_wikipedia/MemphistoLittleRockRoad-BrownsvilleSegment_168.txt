The  Memphis to Little Rock Road-Brownsville Segment  is a historic military road section in  Lonoke County, Arkansas . Located north of  Lonoke  near the hamlet of  Brownsville , the road section was part of the 1828 Memphis to Little Rock Military Road. It was used in the 1830s during the  Trail of Tears  removal of eastern Native American tribes, and was used by military forces on both sides of the  American Civil War  leading up to the  Battle of Brownsville . [2]

The road segment was listed on the  National Register of Historic Places  in 2006. [1]
