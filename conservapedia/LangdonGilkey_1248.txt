Langdon Gilkey  (1918-2004) was a well-respected interpreter of  Reinhold Niebuhr  and  Paul Tillich .  He was also well known for his  science and religion  scholarship, authoring several books on the subject. In the 1980s he became quite  liberal  siding with the  ACLU  in science and religion court battle and exploring traditional eastern religious experiences. [1] [2]
