Previously he served as an attorney in  New York City  and in 1968 he managed Nixon's successful presidential campaign. As Attorney General Mitchell was criticized for increasing telephone wiretaps on private citizens without court authorization, prosecuting  Vietnam War  protesters and attempting to block publication of  Pentagon  Papers.
