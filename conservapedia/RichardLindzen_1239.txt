Richard S. Lindzen  (born February 8, 1940) is an  atmospheric physicist , "renowned for his research in dynamic meteorology - especially atmospheric waves."  [1] 
He was an  Alfred P. Sloan  Professor of  Meteorology  at  Massachusetts Institute of Technology .  He is a vocal critic of  anthropogenic global warming  theories.
 [1]

Richard Lindzen is currently a member of the  National Academy of Sciences  and the Science and Economic Advisory Council of the Annapolis Center for Science-Based Public Policy. He previously held positions at the  University of Chicago  and  Harvard University , and was a contributor to Chapter 4 of the  Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change  2001(IPCC) [2]
