The  1983 United States Embassy bombing  (April 18, 1983) was a  terrorist  attack on a  United States  embassy compound in  Beirut ,  Lebanon . The attack was propagated by a truck bomb, carrying close to one ton of explosives. [1]   Sixty-three people, including the  CIA 's Middle East director, were killed, and 120 were injured.

The bombing was provoked by US intervention in the  1982 Lebanese Civil War .  In 2003, a U.S. District court judge ruled that the attack was carried out by members of  Hezbollah . [2]
