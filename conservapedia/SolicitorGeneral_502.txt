A  Solicitor General  is an individual tasked to conduct all litigation on behalf of the  United States  in the  Supreme Court , and to supervise the handling of litigation in the federal appellate courts. 
The position was mandated by the  1870 Statutory Authorization Act  which stated, "There shall be in the  Department of Justice  an officer learned in the law, to assist the  Attorney General  in the performance of his duties to be called the Solicitor General." [1]
